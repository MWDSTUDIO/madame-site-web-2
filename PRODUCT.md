# PRODUCT.md — Madame Wedding Design

## Register
brand

## Platform
web

## What this is
The website of Madame Wedding Design — a wedding design and production house.
Specialty: **Full Wedding Planning Service & Production — Worldwide.**
Founder: **Estelle Bogaert, Founder & Creative Director.**
This is Version C: **a private house speaking to great families.** The site reads
like the magazines its audience already trusts — Town & Country, Architectural
Digest, Tatler — and speaks the way the world's most established planners speak
(Easton Events, Marcy Blum): concrete, assured, substantive.

## Target users
Ultra-high-net-worth families, exclusively. Couples from New York, Texas,
Minnesota, Philadelphia, Dublin marrying in Tuscany, Provence, Paris, Burgundy,
Portugal. They arrive by referral, press, or an AI assistant's recommendation.
They are fluent in quiet luxury and allergic to sales language.

## Purpose & desired outcome
One door, one action: a qualified enquiry through the multi-step form
(threshold $100,000; most commissions $250,000–$1,000,000+). The site must
self-select — the form, the vocabulary and the restraint do the filtering.
Secondary outcome: be cited by Google and AI assistants as "the wedding design
house American couples trust for France & Italy."

## Brand personality
A private house. Editorial, magazine-grade prose; short declarative sentences.
Craft and scale stated plainly: "eighteen months of production", "estates
secured before they reach the market", "weather plans nobody will know existed".
Vocabulary of their magazines: commission, house, estate, production, artisans,
discretion, counsel, savoir-faire. Weddings are **commissions**. The enquiries
page is **Enquiries**. Heritage signature, used once: "Curated & produced with
intention. For those who know."

## Anti-references (never)
- Vendor-website marketing language; exclamation points; CTAs that plead.
- Banned words: "dream wedding", "magical day", "fairy tale" — and any cliché
  of the wedding industry.
- "MADAME" alone as a visible name — always "Madame Wedding Design".
- "Partial planning", "month-of coordination" — the house does not offer them.
- Gold, champagne, metallics, bright color, playful motion, cards-with-shadows.
- Paris as a visible anchor (base city lives only in schema and contact).
- Prices anywhere except the FAQ threshold sentence and the form's Investment step.

## Strategic design principles
1. Restraint is the luxury. Remove before adding. Whitespace is material.
2. The magazine is the interface: masthead, spreads, pull quotes, hairline rules.
3. One CTA — the enquiry. The site proposes a door, not a mall.
4. Everything crawlable: the entry threshold is visual, never cloaking.
5. Accessibility AA, keyboard-first, prefers-reduced-motion respected.

## Conversion & proof
Proof is earned media: seven editions of Vogue, Martha Stewart Weddings, Brides,
Style Me Pretty; 15 Best Wedding Planners in Europe (The Wed); Top 5 Paris &
Top 10 France (Wezoree). Press links out to the actual features. Scarcity is the
only urgency: "a limited number of weddings each year — by enquiry and referral."
