Castiglion del Bosco, Tuscany

A three-day wedding for Sophie & Gordon, a New York couple with Texas roots, across the thousand-year Rosewood estate in the Val d'Orcia — planned, designed and produced entirely by the house.

The commission

Sophie and Gordon live in New York; the drawl in the toasts came from Texas. They wanted their two worlds seated at one table, in a place that would ask nothing of their guests but presence. We proposed Castiglion del Bosco — a borgo, a church, and five thousand acres of the Val d'Orcia held by a single estate — and built three days around it: an arrival supper in the vineyard, the wedding itself, and a long Sunday lunch nobody wanted to end.

Their guests flew in from four continents. Most had never been to this corner of Tuscany; none had to think about it. Every transfer, room, menu and place card was held by the house.

The production

Eighteen months of production. The estate's calendar was secured before the season opened; the artisans — florists, calligraphers, the string ensemble, the pyrotechnician for one quiet minute of light over the valley — were commissioned by name. Two weather plans existed for every outdoor hour. None were needed; all were rehearsed.

Rosewood Castiglion del Bosco was voted best resort in Europe by Travel + Leisure readers in 2026 — the house has produced two weddings there, and the estate has its own journal entry.
