Portugal

Portugal, in a minimalist key

Marla & Alan asked for restraint: white linen, Atlantic light, and nothing on the table that didn't earn its place. The house produced their Portugal wedding end to end.

The commission

Minimalism is the most demanding brief there is: with nothing to hide behind, every line shows. Marla and Alan wanted a modern, editorial wedding — the kind that photographs like a magazine spread because it was composed like one — on the Portuguese coast.

The production

The house edited rather than added: one long table, one material palette, one uninterrupted view. The discipline sat in what guests never saw — generators buried behind dunes, a wind protocol for every candle, and a schedule built around the Atlantic light. Portugal, and where we would love to produce next there, has its own journal entry.
