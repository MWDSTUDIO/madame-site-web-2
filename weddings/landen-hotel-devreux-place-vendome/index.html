Hôtel d'Évreux, Place Vendôme

An American in Paris: a Texas bride who calls the city home, married in a seventeenth-century private mansion on Place Vendôme, with dinner by Potel & Chabot — produced by the house.

An Hôtel d'Évreux wedding is Paris at its rarest: a seventeenth-century private mansion receiving on Place Vendôme itself. For a Texas bride living in Paris, Madame Wedding Design produced the evening with Potel & Chabot — the city's great caterer since 1820.

The wedding

Landen grew up in Texas and lives in Paris; her wedding had to belong to both. The house proposed Hôtel d'Évreux — the last private mansion on Place Vendôme still receiving — and an evening that read as a dinner at home, if home had gilded boiseries and the column of Vendôme outside the window.

The production

Catering by Potel & Chabot, the caterer of the great Parisian houses since 1820; florals and light directed by the house's Paris ateliers; and the quiet machinery a listed monument demands — load-ins by the courtyard, curfews kept, neighbors greeted by name. The venue has its own journal entry.
