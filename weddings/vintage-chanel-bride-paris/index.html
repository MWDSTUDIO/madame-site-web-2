Paris — the vintage-Chanel bride

A Paris wedding composed around a pink vintage Chanel gown that once walked the runway on Claudia Schiffer — produced by the house and published in seven editions of Vogue, from London to Sydney.

A Paris wedding built around a vintage Chanel wedding dress asks for more than planning: couture handling, closed venues, and press on the bride's terms. Produced by Madame Wedding Design, this celebration became one of the most published Paris weddings of its year — seven editions of Vogue, from London to Sydney.

The wedding

The gown came first: a pink vintage Chanel, runway-worn by Claudia Schiffer, found and secured long before a date existed. The commission was to build a Paris evening worthy of it — rooms, light, flowers and paper composed in the gown's own register, nothing louder than the dress.

The bride wished the celebration private until she chose otherwise. When she did, Vogue told the story — in seven editions.

The production

A production measured in fittings as much as in floor plans: the gown's conservation set the temperature of the rooms, the length of the processional, even the hour of the photographs. The house directed every artisan around a single object and a single idea, and handled the press on the family's terms — which is why the coverage reads like a portfolio, not an exposure.
