Shangri-La Paris

A palace evening above the Seine for Jessica & Chris, an American couple — opulent and intimate at once, produced by the house and featured in Brides.

The commission

Jessica and Chris wanted Paris at its most assured: a former Bonaparte residence, the Eiffel Tower filling the windows, and a guest list small enough that every seat mattered. The brief was intimacy at palace scale — a room that felt inevitable rather than arranged.

The production

The house closed the historic salons, rebuilt their lighting for candle height, and ran the evening on a minute-by-minute score — ceremony, dinner, and a first dance timed to the tower's own lights. Brides called the result opulent and intimate in the same sentence, which was precisely the commission.
