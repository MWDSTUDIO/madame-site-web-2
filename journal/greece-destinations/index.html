Destinations — Greece

Greece, considered: Amanzoe and the Athens Riviera

Greece is on the house's drawing table — stated plainly as desire, not history. Two addresses lead the study: Amanzoe, above Porto Heli, and the Four Seasons Astir Palace on the Athens Riviera.

Why Greece, and why now

Because the country has quietly assembled what our families ask for elsewhere: closed estates, serious kitchens, and light that needs no design. The Aegean evening — that long violet hour — is a production value no budget can buy in the north.

Where we would love to produce

Aspirational, and framed as such: the house has not yet produced in Greece. These are the venues we have studied for the first commission that takes us there.

Amanzoe, Porto Heli

Aman's hilltop acropolis above the Peloponnese coast — pavilions around a colonnaded axis, villas with their own pools, and a beach club below. Aman discipline plus Greek light: for the family that wants serenity produced to the millimeter.

Four Seasons Astir Palace, Athens Riviera

The storied Astir peninsula, thirty minutes from the Acropolis — pine terraces over the sea, a private-bay ceremony, and the city's great hotels holding the guest list. Athens gives a wedding a capital's depth with a coastline's ease.

Planning a Greek wedding from abroad

The playbook the house runs in Italy and France transfers whole: secure the estate first, produce locally with vetted teams, choreograph arrivals through Athens, and let the place carry what it already does best. When the first Greek commission comes, it will be planned exactly so.
