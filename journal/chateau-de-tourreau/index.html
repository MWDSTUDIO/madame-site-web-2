Château de Tourreau: a private estate wedding in Provence

Château de Tourreau, near Sarrians, is an eighteenth-century château with one of the great private gardens of the Vaucluse — fountains, allées and a pool court built for long evenings.

Why Château de Tourreau

Because it is Provence with its collar up: formal gardens instead of rustic lavender, stone the color of late honey, and an estate that closes completely to hold one family in residence. The gardens sequence a wedding by themselves — arrival by the fountain court, ceremony in the allées, dinner under the plane trees.

What we produced here

The house has produced in the Vaucluse at Tourreau's register — exclusive-use estates, multi-day formats, artisan tables under old trees — and counts the château among the addresses it returns to with intent.

Planning your wedding at Château de Tourreau

Reserve the estate for several nights and let it work as a residence: guests staying on site turn a wedding into a house party, which is Provence's native format. The mistral is real — the house designs every candle and canopy for it.

The estate in brief

  • Sarrians, Vaucluse — between Avignon and the Ventoux
  • Eighteenth-century château; formal private gardens, fountains, pool court
  • Exclusive use; sleeps the core wedding party
