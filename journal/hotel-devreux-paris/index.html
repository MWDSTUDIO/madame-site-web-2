Hôtel d'Évreux: a private mansion on Place Vendôme

Hôtel d'Évreux is the rarest kind of Paris venue: a seventeenth-century private mansion still receiving on Place Vendôme itself. The house produced an American bride's wedding here, with Potel & Chabot at the stoves.

Why Hôtel d'Évreux

Because it is a home, not a hotel — gilded boiseries, a courtyard of honor, and windows that open onto the Vendôme column. A dinner here reads as a private invitation to the most guarded square in Paris, and the mansion's scale (one great salon, a handful of rooms around it) keeps every guest inside the same evening.

What we produced here

Landen's wedding — a Texas bride who calls Paris home — with catering by Potel & Chabot, caterer to the great Parisian houses since 1820. A listed monument demands its own choreography: courtyard load-ins, curfews kept to the minute, neighbors greeted by name. That choreography is the commission.

Planning your wedding at Hôtel d'Évreux

Think in evenings, not days — the mansion is at its best from the first candle to a midnight departure across the square. Pair it with a welcome dinner elsewhere in the city and let Vendôme be the crescendo.
