Shangri-La Paris: a palace wedding above the Seine

Shangri-La Paris is Prince Roland Bonaparte's former residence, and its salons face the Eiffel Tower squarely. The house produced Jessica & Chris's wedding here — the evening Brides called opulent and intimate at once.

Why Shangri-La Paris

Because no other palace in Paris holds the tower that close: from La Suite Shangri-La and the historic salons, the Eiffel Tower is not a view, it is a guest. The building's Bonaparte bones — restored ceilings, imperial ironwork — give even a small wedding the weight of an era.

What we produced here

Jessica & Chris's wedding: historic salons closed to the public, lighting rebuilt to candle height, and a running order timed to the tower's own sparkle. Brides published the evening.

Planning your wedding at Shangri-La Paris

Book the salons early and decide at once what the tower will witness — ceremony, dinner or first dance; the whole design flows from that choice. The palace's own teams are superb, and they work best with a production house that brings them a finished score.
