Castiglion del Bosco: weddings at the best resort in Europe

Rosewood Castiglion del Bosco — a thousand-year borgo on five thousand acres of the Val d'Orcia — was voted best resort in Europe by Travel + Leisure readers in 2026, scoring 99.58 out of 100. Madame Wedding Design has produced two weddings there.

Why Castiglion del Bosco

Because it solves the destination wedding's central problem: how to hold a hundred people for three days without the seams showing. The estate is a former Medici property restored by Massimo and Chiara Ferragamo — a borgo with its own church, suites and villas enough to sleep an entire wedding, a winery, and a valley protected by UNESCO on every horizon. In Travel + Leisure's World's Best Awards 2026, readers rated it the best resort in Europe — 99.58 out of 100.

What we produced here

Sophie & Gordon's three-day celebration — a New York couple with Texas roots, an arrival supper in the vineyard, the wedding in the borgo, a Sunday lunch that refused to end. And Agatha's thirteen-guest wedding in a private villa, which received the same production depth at a fraction of the headcount. Two commissions, one lesson: the estate rewards houses that plan in months, not weeks.

Planning your wedding at Castiglion del Bosco

Secure the estate first — its calendar closes a year or more out — and build the design around what is already perfect: the valley, the stone, the wine. Guests fly into Florence or Rome; the house choreographs the rest. Budget for exclusivity where it matters (the borgo, the church hour, the spa mornings) and let the Val d'Orcia carry the views it has carried for a millennium.
