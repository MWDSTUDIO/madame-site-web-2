Château Martinay: vines, stone and a Provence weekend

Château Martinay sits among its own vines beneath Mont Ventoux — a working estate that hosts a wedding the way it hosts a harvest: with room, rhythm and golden hour on its side.

Why Château Martinay

Because a wine estate gives a weekend its own logic: the vines set the horizon, the cellars set the menu, and the courtyard holds a dinner the way a cloister holds quiet. Martinay is Provence without performance — which is the hardest Provence to find.

What we produced here

A weekend for a Minnesota family, produced quietly and kept unpublished at their wish — NDAs across the vendor team, a closed perimeter, no photography released. The commission page tells what may be told.

Planning your wedding at Château Martinay

Build around the estate's day: a morning in the vines, a slow afternoon, dinner at the exact hour the Ventoux turns violet. Guests stay between the château and the villages nearby; the house runs the transfers so the country feels near, never far.

The estate in brief

  • Provence — beneath Mont Ventoux, among its own vineyards
  • Exclusive-use château; courtyard and cellar dinners
  • Produced here by the house: one (unpublished) commission
