Château de Villette: the little Versailles, closed for one family

Forty minutes from Paris, Château de Villette — they call it le Petit Versailles — closes entirely for one family at a time: grand-siècle interiors, water parterres, and gardens in the Le Nôtre tradition.

Why Château de Villette

Because it offers what Paris proper cannot: seventeenth-century grandeur with total privacy. The château sleeps a wedding party, the gardens hold ceremony, cocktails and fireworks without ever crowding, and the water parterre gives photographers the mirror they cross oceans for.

What we produced here

The house has produced at Villette with the estate closed end to end — ceremony at the parterre, dinner in the Grand Salon, breakfast under the orangery's limes. Style Me Pretty has covered the house's work; Villette's own pages are coming to the portfolio.

Planning your wedding at Château de Villette

Take the château for the full weekend — its value is in waking up there, not visiting. Paris hotels hold the overflow guests; the house shuttles the distance so smoothly nobody measures it.

The estate in brief

  • Condécourt, Val-d'Oise — 40 minutes from Paris
  • Gardens in the Le Nôtre tradition; interiors of the grand siècle
  • Exclusive-use estate; sleeps the wedding party on site
