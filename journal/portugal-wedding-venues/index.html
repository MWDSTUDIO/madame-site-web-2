Destinations — Portugal

Portugal: where we have produced, and where we would love to next

The house has produced on Portugal's Atlantic coast — Marla & Alan's minimalist wedding — and keeps three addresses on its drawing table: Sublime Comporta, Tivoli Palácio de Seteais, and Six Senses Douro Valley.

What we produced here

Marla & Alan's wedding: a modern, editorial celebration in a minimalist key — one long table, one material palette, and the Atlantic light doing what no lighting designer can. Portugal met the brief with its particular grace: direct flights, generous estates, and a hospitality culture built for long celebrations.

Where we would love to produce next

These are aspirations, stated plainly as such — venues we have studied, visited or costed, and would delight in producing at.

Sublime Comporta

Cabins and villas scattered through umbrella pines an hour south of Lisbon, with rice fields running to the dunes. For the family that wants barefoot to mean designed-barefoot: the hardest register to fake, the easiest to love.

Tivoli Palácio de Seteais, Sintra

An eighteenth-century palace in the Sintra hills — frescoed ballrooms, box parterres, and the Atlantic haze that made poets of the English. The formal counterpoint to Comporta's linen.

Six Senses Douro Valley

A renovated nineteenth-century manor above the terraced Douro: harvest-season dinners, river light, and port from the slopes in view of the table.

Planning a Portugal wedding from abroad

Portugal may be the most gracious European destination to plan from the United States — direct flights to Lisbon and Porto, English-fluent estates, and seasons that stretch the calendar. The house contracts locally and produces on the ground, with wind protocols for every candle on that beautiful, insistent coast.
