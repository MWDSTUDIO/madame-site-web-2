Venues — Europe

A celebration aboard the Venice Simplon-Orient-Express

The Venice Simplon-Orient-Express is the only venue in Europe that moves: restored 1920s carriages, a horizon that changes by the course, and a production schedule written in minutes rather than hours.

Why the Orient Express

Because it turns transit — the dead time of every destination wedding — into the celebration itself. Thirty guests, marquetry and mohair, black tie at teatime, and Europe sliding past the window between courses. No other format concentrates a group so completely: nobody leaves a moving train.

What we produced here

A private celebration across the train's restored carriages — boarding choreography on the platform, a pianist in the bar car, menus sequenced to the landscape outside. A moving venue rehearses differently: every timing is the railway's, and the production must be fitted to it like couture.

Planning your celebration aboard

Think of the train as the destination and the cities at each end as its anterooms — a dinner in Paris before, a morning in Venice after. Charters of carriages, or the full train, are contracted far in advance; luggage, dress codes and quiet logistics are the house's craft at their purest.

The train in brief

  • Restored 1920s carriages; routes across Paris, Venice, the Alps
  • Private carriage or full-train charters; intimate guest lists
  • The house's most compressed production schedule — and its most shareable evening
